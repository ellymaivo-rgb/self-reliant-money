Lesson 2: Health Insurance Essentials

üè• Understanding Health Insurance

Health insurance is the most critical insurance for families. Medical bills are the #1 cause of bankruptcy in America.

üè¢ Employer-Sponsored Plans

  • Usually cheapest option
  • Employer pays 70-80% of premium
  • Limited to employer's choices
  • May not cover family optimally

üõí Marketplace Plans (ACA)

  • More plan options
  • Subsidies available based on income
  • Essential health benefits required
  • Cannot deny for pre-existing conditions

üí∞ Key Terms & Costs

Term Definition Impact
Premium Monthly payment Budget for this fixed cost
Deductible You pay first $X per year Higher deductible = lower premium
Copay Fixed cost per visit $25-50 typical for office visits
Out-of-pocket max Maximum you pay per year Catastrophic protection limit

Lesson 3: Life Insurance Guide

üíô Life Insurance Purpose

Life insurance replaces income when the primary earner dies, ensuring family can maintain their lifestyle and meet financial obligations.

üí∞ Income Replacement

  • Replace 70-80% of lost income
  • Duration: Until children independent
  • Consider inflation and raises

üéì Education Funding

  • College costs: $25,000-75,000 per child
  • Private school tuition
  • Educational support expenses

üè† Debt Coverage

  • Mortgage balance
  • Credit cards and loans
  • Final expenses ($10,000-15,000)

üìä Term vs Whole Life Comparison

Feature Term Life Whole Life
Cost Very affordable 10-20x more expensive
Coverage period 10, 20, 30 years Lifetime
Cash value None Builds slowly
Best for Young families Specific estate planning

üí° Expert Recommendation

Buy term life insurance and invest the difference. Term is 90% cheaper, allowing you to invest the savings for better returns than whole life's cash value.

üßÆ How Much Coverage Do You Need?

Simple Formula:

Coverage = (Annual Income √ó 10) + Debts + Education Costs

Example for $60,000 income family:

  • Income replacement: $60,000 √ó 10 = $600,000
  • Mortgage balance: $200,000
  • College fund (2 kids): $100,000
  • Total needed: $900,000

Lesson 4: Property & Auto Insurance

üè† Homeowners/Renters Insurance

üè° Homeowners Insurance

  • Dwelling coverage: Rebuild cost (not market value)
  • Personal property: Contents replacement
  • Liability: Injuries on your property
  • Additional living: Hotel costs during repairs

üè¢ Renters Insurance

  • Personal property: Your belongings
  • Liability: Accidents you cause
  • Additional living: Temporary housing
  • Very affordable: $15-30/month typical

üöó Auto Insurance Essentials

üö® Required by Law

  • Liability: Damage you cause to others
  • Minimum limits: Often insufficient
  • Recommendation: 100/300/100 minimums

üõ°Ô∏è Optional but Important

  • Collision: Damage to your car
  • Comprehensive: Theft, weather, vandalism
  • Uninsured motorist: Protection from others without insurance

üìà Raise Deductibles

$500 ‚Üí $1,000 deductible can save 15-30% on premiums

üõ°Ô∏è Maintain Good Credit

üîç Shop Around

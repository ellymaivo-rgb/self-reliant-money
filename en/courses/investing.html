Lesson 1: Why Should You Invest?

üéØ Inflation Erodes Your Savings

If you only keep money in savings accounts with 4-5% annual interest, while annual inflation is around 3-4%, your money only grows very little in real value.

Example: $10,000 today, after 10 years with 4% annual inflation, will only have purchasing power equivalent to $6,700.

üí° The Power of Compound Interest

Early investment helps you leverage the power of compound interest. With average returns of 8-10% annually, your money can double every 7-9 years.

  • Invest $500/month from age 28 ‚Üí $580,000 at age 55
  • Invest $500/month from age 35 ‚Üí $320,000 at age 55
  • 7-year difference = $260,000 loss!

üéØ Long-term Financial Goals

Investment helps you achieve important goals:

  • Buy a house in 5-7 years
  • Children's college fund ($100,000-200,000)
  • Comfortable retirement with $500,000-1,000,000
  • Financial freedom earlier

Lesson 2: Safe Investment Principles

üõ°Ô∏è 5 Golden Rules

  1. Invest only surplus money: Ensure you have 6-month emergency fund
  2. Understand before investing: Never invest in what you don't understand
  3. Diversify: Don't put all eggs in one basket
  4. Long-term investment: Minimum 5 years to reduce short-term risks
  5. Control emotions: Don't buy due to FOMO, don't sell due to panic

‚ö†Ô∏è Risk vs Return

Investment Channel Risk Expected Return Suitable For
Bank savings Very low 4-6% annually Emergency fund
Government bonds Low 5-7% annually Conservative investment
Mutual funds Medium 8-12% annually Long-term investment
Stocks High 10-15% annually Experienced investors

Lesson 3: Stocks - Owning Businesses

üìà What are stocks?

Stocks are certificates proving you own a portion of a company. When the company grows and increases profits, your stock value also increases.

üí∞

High long-term returns

History shows stocks provide 8-12% annually long-term

üìä

Regular dividends

Many companies pay 3-6% annual dividends to shareholders

üõ°Ô∏è

Inflation protection

Value increases over time, protecting purchasing power

üéØ How to choose stocks for beginners

‚úÖ Basic criteria

  • Large companies: S&P 500, Apple, Microsoft...
  • Stable industries: Banking, consumer goods, utilities
  • Consistent profits: Stable growth for 3-5 years
  • Low debt: Debt-to-equity ratio < 50%

üöÄ Dollar Cost Averaging (DCA) Strategy

Instead of buying once, divide money into regular purchases:

  • Buy $200-300 worth of stocks monthly
  • Choose 2-3 reliable stock symbols
  • Reduce risk from price volatility

Lesson 4: Mutual Funds - Expert Management

üè¶ What are mutual funds?

Mutual funds pool money from many investors for professional management. Perfect for busy people or those without experience.

üìä Stock Funds

  • Invest mainly in stocks
  • High profit potential
  • Higher risk
  • Management fee: 1.5-2.5% annually

üèõÔ∏è Bond Funds

  • Invest in bonds
  • Stable returns
  • Lower risk
  • Management fee: 0.5-1.5% annually

‚öñÔ∏è Balanced Funds

  • Combine stocks + bonds
  • Moderate risk-return
  • Suitable for beginners
  • Management fee: 1-2% annually

üéØ How to choose mutual funds

1

Determine goals

  • Short-term (< 3 years): Bond funds
  • Medium-term (3-7 years): Balanced funds
  • Long-term (> 7 years): Stock funds
2

Compare performance

  • Review 3-5 year returns
  • Compare with market index
  • Include management fees
3

Choose reputable companies

  • Vanguard, Fidelity, BlackRock
  • Charles Schwab, T. Rowe Price
  • Experience > 5 years

Lesson 5: Real Estate - Tangible Assets

üè† Real estate investment: Pros & cons

‚úÖ Advantages

  • Tangible assets, easy to understand
  • Good inflation protection
  • Can rent for income
  • Easy bank financing

‚ùå Disadvantages

  • Requires large initial capital
  • Poor liquidity (hard to sell quickly)
  • High transaction costs (2-10%)
  • Complex legal risks

üéØ Real estate investment options

üèòÔ∏è Buy to live

Priority #1 for young couples

  • No rent payments
  • Build long-term assets
  • Stable lifestyle

üè¢ Buy to rent

Requires large capital, much experience

  • Rental income 4-8% annually
  • Price appreciation 5-10% annually
  • Risks: Hard to rent, price decline

üìú REITs

Real estate investment with small money

  • Start from $1,000
  • High liquidity
  • Regular dividend payments

üí° Strategy for young couples

25-30 years old

Accumulation phase

  • Focus on saving for first home
  • Invest in stocks/funds for rapid growth
  • Don't borrow for investment real estate yet
30-35 years old

Home buying phase

  • Buy home to live in (60-70% loan)
  • Maintain stock/fund investments
  • Consider REITs if have surplus money

Lesson 6: Building Balanced Investment Portfolio

‚öñÔ∏è Asset allocation principles

Diversification reduces risk while maintaining returns. "Don't put all eggs in one basket"

üöÄ Aggressive model (25-35 years old)

Stocks/Stock funds: 60%
Bonds/Savings: 20%
Real estate: 15%
Emergency fund: 5%

üõ°Ô∏è Conservative model (35-45 years old)

Stocks/Stock funds: 40%
Bonds/Savings: 35%
Real estate: 20%
Emergency fund: 5%

üìä Portfolio rebalancing

Why rebalance?

Over time, asset proportions change due to price movements. Need to adjust back to original ratios.

‚è∞ When to rebalance?

  • Periodically: Every 6 months or annually
  • Threshold-based: When deviation > 5% from target
  • Life events: Income changes, goal changes

üìù Rebalancing example

Target: 60% stocks, 40% bonds

Current: 70% stocks (price increase), 30% bonds

Action: Sell some stocks, buy more bonds

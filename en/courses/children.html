üì± Ages 11-14: Financial Skills

Lesson 5: Child Tax Benefits

üéÅ Child Tax Credit

The Child Tax Credit can significantly reduce your tax burden.

üí∞ Credit Amount

  • $2,000 per child under 17
  • $500 per dependent 17-24 (full-time student)
  • Up to $1,500 refundable per child

üìä Income Limits (2024)

  • Single filers: Phases out at $200,000
  • Married filing jointly: Phases out at $400,000
  • Reduces by $50 for every $1,000 over limit

üë∂ Child and Dependent Care Credit

Qualifying Expenses:

  • Daycare and preschool
  • After-school programs
  • Summer day camps
  • Babysitter expenses (while working)

üí∞ Credit Calculation

Income Level Credit Rate Max Credit
Under $15,000 35% $2,100 (1 child), $4,200 (2+)
$15,000-$43,000 35-20% Sliding scale
Over $43,000 20% $1,200 (1 child), $2,400 (2+)

üìö Education Tax Benefits

üéì American Opportunity Credit

  • Up to $2,500 per student per year
  • First 4 years of college
  • 40% refundable (up to $1,000)
  • Covers tuition, fees, books, supplies

üìñ Lifetime Learning Credit

  • Up to $2,000 per tax return
  • No limit on number of years
  • Covers tuition and fees only
  • For undergraduate, graduate, professional courses

üõ°Ô∏è Life Insurance for Parents

Having children dramatically increases your life insurance needs.

üìä Coverage Calculation with Children

Base Coverage

10x annual income

+ Child Expenses

$250,000 per child (birth to independence)

+ Education Costs

$100,000-300,000 per child

+ Outstanding Debts

Mortgage, loans, credit cards

Example: Family with 2 children

  • Income replacement: $75,000 √ó 10 = $750,000
  • Child expenses: $250,000 √ó 2 = $500,000
  • Education costs: $150,000 √ó 2 = $300,000

üìÑ Legal Documents for Parents

üë• Guardian Designation

  • Discuss with chosen guardians first
  • Name backup guardians

üí∞ Financial Guardian

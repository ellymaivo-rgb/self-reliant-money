Emergency Fund Essentials

üö® What Qualifies as Emergency

True emergencies are unexpected, necessary, and urgent expenses that you cannot plan for.

  • Job loss or reduced income
  • Major medical expenses
  • Essential home repairs
  • Car repairs for work commute

üí° Where to Keep It

Emergency funds should be easily accessible but separate from daily spending accounts.

  • High-yield savings account
  • Money market account
  • Short-term CDs (if accessible)
  • NOT in checking or investment accounts

üìà Building Your Fund

Start small and build consistently rather than waiting to save large amounts.

  • Start with $500-1000 mini fund
  • Save windfalls (tax refunds, bonuses)
  • Cut non-essential expenses temporarily
  • Consider side income to accelerate

üîÑ Maintaining Your Fund

Once built, maintain and protect your emergency fund for true emergencies only.

  • Replenish after any use
  • Adjust for lifestyle changes
  • Review annually and increase if needed
  • Don't use for vacations or wants
